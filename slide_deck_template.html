Investigation Overview¶

In this investigation of the Prosper Loan Data, I will investigate the relationship between Loan Amount, Loan Terms, and Interest Rates

Dataset Overview¶

This data set contains 113,937 loans with 81 variables on each loan, including loan amount, borrower rate (or interest rate), current loan status, borrower income, and many others.

Investigating the Relationship between Loan Amount and Interest Rate¶

Now let's take a look at the relationship between the other 2 variables with a scatter plot. Notice that Loan amounts may seem categorical with significantly more values plotted at each of the milestone amounts (5,000, 10,000, 15,000 and so on). It's important to remember that the loan amount values are still numerical and only looks this way due to loans being offered at nice numbers (ending in 0s).

We can clearly see the moderate negative correlation Loan Amount has with the interest rate. While smaller loans (< 10000) are capping out at around 35% interest rate, borrowing 35k is only capping at around 15%. In between these loan amounts, you can see this downward trend very clearly. In fact, the highest amount of interest for someone borrowing 35k is mere 18.19%.

Investigating the Relationship between Credit Score, Loan Amount, and Interest Rate¶

A few very interesting insights can be gained here. First, 12 month term loans have the lowest interest rate and is by far the least occuring loan term. The highest recorded 12 month term loan was also 25k. Secondly, we see that based on the opacity of the general graph, 36 term loans are the most frequent - and it only makes sense that the loaners try to make their most money from this group of borrowers by charging them the highest interest across all loans. Across all loan amounts up to 30k, we see that 36 month term loans have the highest interest rates. In the last graph, we see that 60 month loans are the second-most popular loan type. They generally offer a lower interest across the board, except bigger loans, starting at 30k and up, with marginal increase in interest rates.
